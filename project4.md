# Ergonomic Dictophone 

This is my submission for the third project in this class: creating a touch sensitive interface with an ESP32, copper tape, wire and my laptop.

I was inspired to make an ergonomic dictophone when having dinner with my family. My brother is a resident in radiology, and works behind a computer for 10+ hours a day, sometimes 7 days a week, reading scans and talking into his dictophone. He complains a lot about wrist pain from the constant computer work and mouse movement, even though he uses an ergonomic mouse and keyboard. One thing I feel could be more ergonomic is the dictophone he uses, since you have to press a button to start recording. I thought that maybe making it into a touch sensitive pad would be less stress for the wrist (I know touching a button doesn't seem like a lot of stress for the wrist, but I think doing it constantly for so many hours in a day every day does take its toll on your wrist).

For my ergonomic dictophone, since it's a device, I wanted it to be a little box with the ESP32 inside, and the outside just has some pads of copper tape for the buttons. It needed 4 buttons: an on/off button, a record/stop button, a pause/play button, and a button that switches what mode the device is in(options are raw voice recording or voice-to-text).
